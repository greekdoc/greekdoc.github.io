Ἀτάδ
  • Meaning: Atad
  • Note: Place where Joseph led his brothers back to Canaan to bury Jacob (Gen 50:10)
Ἀταΐμ
  • Meaning: Ataim
  • Note: Name of a city/town, but corresponding Hebrew word means "plants"
  • Concord: 1Chron 4:23
Ἀτάρ
  • Meaning: Atar, Jatal
  • Note: Ancestor of gatekeepers (1Esdras 5:28)
Ἀτάρα
  • Meaning: Atarah
  • Note: Wife of Jerahmeel (1Chron 2:26)
Ἀταρώθ
  • Meaning: Ataroth
  • Note: Ataroth: One of the leaders of the tribes of Reuben and Gad who wanted to stay in the land of Jazer and Gilead (Num 32:3)
  • Note: Ataroth: place on the boundary between Ephraim and Benjamin, toward the West (Josh 16:5). Also called Ataroth-Addar
  • Note: Atroth Beth Joab: Probably a family in Judah (1Chron 2:54)
Ἀτεργατειόν
  • Parse: Noun: Nom/Acc Sing Neut
  • Meaning: Atargatis
  • Note: Name of a goddess (2Macc 12:26)
Ἀτήρ
  • Meaning: Ater
  • Note: Son of Hezekias whose descendants were led out of captivity (Ezra 2:16; 1Esdras 5:15)
  • Note: One of the leaders of the people who endorsed Nehemiah's covenant (Neh 10:18)
Ἀτητά
  • Meaning: Teta, Ateta
  • Note: Gatekeeper family (1Esdras 5:28)
Ἀτθαρίας
  • Parse: Noun: Nom Sing Masc
  • Meaning: Attharias
  • Note: A leader with Nehemiah (1Esdras 5:40)
Ἀτίλ
  • Meaning: Hattil
  • Note: Servants of Solomon who came out of Babylon with Zerubbabel (Ezra 2:57)
Ἀτιτά
  • Meaning: Hatita
  • Note: Gatekeeper in days of Ezra (Ezra 2:42)
Ἀτιφά
  • Meaning: Hatipha
  • Note: Head of family of those who came out of captivity (1Esdras 5:32)
Ἀτούς
  • Meaning: Hattush
  • Note: Descendant of David. Priest who endorsed Nehemiah's covenant (Neh 10:5)
  • Note: Father of Jedaiah (Neh 3:10)
Ἀτουφά
  • Meaning: Hatipha
  • Note: Family of Nethinim who came out of Babylon with Zerubbabel (Ezra 2:54)
Ἀττάλεια
  • Parse: Noun: Nom Sing Fem
  • Meaning: Attalia
  • Note: City of Pamphylia (Acts 14:25)
  • Forms:
    • Ἀττάλειαν Noun: Acc Sing Fem
Ἄτταλος
  • Parse: Noun: Nom Sing Masc

  • Note: King of Pergamum (1Macc 15:22)
  • Forms:
    • Ἄτταλον Noun: Acc Sing Masc
    • Ἀττάλῳ Noun: Dat Sing Masc
